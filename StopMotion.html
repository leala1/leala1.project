Technology in Stop-Motion Animation

URL: Stop-Motion Animation Looks Old School, but Its Tech Is Incredible
Site Author Ian Failes
Abstract This site has a list of five different technology advancements that are used in stop motion films. With each different advancement, Failes includes an example of a film to show how the technology is used behind the scenes. Starts with 3D printing and how it is helpful in creating all kinds of facial expressions. Then talks about how computer graphics and digital visual effects made their way into stop-motion films. The site then goes into depth of Go-motion, Willis O'Brien and Ray Harryhausen, and claymation.

URL: Digital Age of Stop Motion
Site Author Lilah Bricker
Abstract The site talks about how stop-motion films use more and more digital graphics in films. Starts off with talking about how digital cameras and rigs have changed the outcome of films. Site then talks about how programs came out of rig such as Aardman and Laika. The site then talks about different stop motion movies and how CGI was used in them. They start with mentioning Flushed Away (2006) and how it was created entirely of CGI but was animated with flaws to make it look realistic. The site then talks about The Lego Movie and Boxtrolls.
